# **MANUAL OF INSTRUCTIONS**

## **Consistent Communicators Club (CC Club)**

## **1\. Introduction**

The **Consistent Communicators Club (CC Club)** is a structured, campus-based communication development program designed to transform students into **confident speakers and placement-ready professionals**.

This manual provides **step-by-step operational guidelines** for colleges to ensure smooth implementation, monitoring, and success of the program.

## **2\. Objective of the Program**

- Improve **English communication fluency**
- Build **confidence in speaking**
- Prepare students for **interviews & placements**
- Develop **presentation and leadership skills**
- Create a **consistent practice ecosystem**

## **3\. Institutional Onboarding**

### **3.1 Registration**

- Register on the **CC Club platform** (to be accessed from theconsistentacademy.in website but will have a different webpage/UI/functionality)
- Provide institutional details
- Receive login credentials

### **3.2 Appointment of Faculty Coordinator**

Each college must appoint **1 Faculty Coordinator**

**Responsibilities:**

- Act as the **primary point of contact**
- Monitor club activities
- Ensure discipline and continuity
- Coordinate with The Consistent Academy

## **4\. Student Enrollment**

- Students must register via the platform
- **Mandatory participation recommended**

## **5\. Club Structure & Roles**

### **5.1 Leadership Roles**

| **Role**        | **Selection**       | **Responsibility**            |
| --------------- | ------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| President       | Selected by Academy | Lead meetings, upload reports |
| Vice President  | Selected by Academy | Assist President              |
| Team Leader     | Selected internally | Manage teams                  |
| Event Team      | Volunteers          | Conduct activities            |
| Trainer Buddies | Level 2 qualified   | Support training              |

## **6\. Training & Induction**

All selected members will receive training from **The Consistent Academy**:

- Leadership training
- Meeting execution training
- Activity management
- Evaluation awareness

## **7\. Weekly Working Model**

### **7.1 Meeting Frequency**

- **2 meetings per week**
- **1 ONLINE - (GRAMMAR SESSIONS /1:1 MOCK INTERVIEW)**
- **1 offline meeting mandatory ( ON THE CAMPUS )**

### **7.2 Offline Meeting Structure**

Each meeting must follow this format:

#### **Part 1: Prepared Speeches**

- 2-minute speeches by students

#### **Part 2: Speaking Activities**

- Group Discussions
- Role Plays
- Extempore
- Games

#### **Part 3: Inter Departmental / inter college Speech competitions**

## **8\. Documentation & Reporting**

Access to the admin page of the CC Club will be given to the student president.
After every offline meeting, the President must upload:

- Minutes of Meeting
- Attendance at the meeting
- 1 short video **(CAPTURING FLICKS THE WHOLE MEETING)**
- 2-3 photos **( INCLUDING A GROUP PHOTO)**

Upload on the **CC Club platform**

## **9\. Speech Framework (Mandatory)**

- **16 speeches in all**
- **The students try to complete all the speeches as soon as they can and take up leadership roles for brownie points**
- Each speech must be:
  - Recorded (2 minutes)
  - Uploaded on the platform

## **10\. Speech Evaluation System**

### **10.1 Backend Evaluation**

- All speeches are evaluated by the **The Consistent Academy team**

### **10.2 Outcomes**

#### Well Done

- Student passes
- Marks awarded

#### Redo

- Student must:
  - Improve performance
  - Re-record and re-upload

  **Student must continue until "Well Done" is achieved**

## **11\. Level-Based Progression**

The program consists of **4 Levels**:

### **Level 1 Criteria**

- 80% attendance
- 4 speeches
- 1 test

### **Level 2, 3, 4**

- Same as level 1
- Increasing difficulty
- Advanced communication skills

## **12\. Certification Requirement**

- Students must complete **all 4 levels**
- **4 certificates required before placement interviews**

## **13\. Placement Integration**

- Only students with **minimum Level 3 certification** should be allowed for placements _(recommended)_
- Level 4 students are considered **highly proficient communicators**

## **14\. Scoring System**

### **14.1 Role-Based Points**

These points will be awarded to the leaders only if there is smooth execution of the club -( conducting proper meeting, maintaining decorum , proper documentation etc)

| **Role**       | **Points** |
| -------------- | ---------- |
| President      | 10         |
| Vice President | 7          |
| Team Leader    | 5          |
| Event Team     | 3          |

### **14.2 Total Score Includes**

- Attendance
- Test scores
- Speech performance
- Leadership role points

## **15\. Badge System**

- Each Leader will have a badge
- Each level has a **distinct badge**
- Distributed during offline meetings
- Builds **accountability and identity**

## **16\. Compliance Requirements**

To ensure active participation:

- Conduct **weekly meetings regularly**
- Maintain **attendance records**
- Upload **all required documentation**
- Ensure **student participation**

## **17\. Infrastructure Requirements**

- Classroom or seminar hall
- Basic recording device (mobile)
- Internet access

## **18\. Monitoring & Support**

The Consistent Academy will:

- Track student progress
- Review uploaded content
- Provide feedback
- Offer technical and academic support

## **19\. Discipline & Quality Control**

- Students must maintain:
  - Attendance
  - Participation
  - Timely submissions
- Colleges must ensure:
  - Proper execution
  - No fake uploads
  - Authentic participation

## **20\. Expected Outcomes**

The CC Club ensures that:

- **English speaking environment** in the department or college
- Every student **speaks English**
- Every student i**mproves in their articulation**
- **Leadership-ready students**
- **Confident interview performers**
- Every student becomes **placement-ready.**

## **21\. Conclusion**

The **Consistent Communicators Club** is not just a club-it is a **systematic transformation process** that equips students with **lifelong communication skills**.

# **CC CLUB - SPEECH FRAMEWORK (LEVEL 1 to LEVEL 4)**

**_4 Speeches per Semester | Progressive Difficulty_**

## **LEVEL 1 - FOUNDATION (Comfort & Clarity)**

_Goal: Remove fear, build basic speaking habit_

### **Speech 1: Self-Introduction Speech**

**Objective:** To speak about oneself with clarity and basic confidence.

**What to Prepare:**

- Name, background, interests
- Goals and aspirations
- Simple structured delivery

**Example Topics:**

- "Who am I?"
- "My Journey So Far"
- "My Strengths and Dreams"

### **Speech 2: My Daily Life / Routine**

**Objective:** To improve sentence formation and fluency in everyday English.

**What to Prepare:**

- Daily schedule
- Habits and routines
- Use of simple tenses

**Example Topics:**

- "A Day in My Life"
- "My Productive Routine"
- "My Weekend Story"

### **Speech 3: My Favourite**

**Objective:** To express likes and opinions with basic vocabulary.

**What to Prepare:**

- Describe a favorite person/place/movie/book
- Give reasons

**Example Topics:**

- "My Favourite Teacher"
- "A Movie I Love"
- "My Favourite Place"

### **Speech 4: Describe an Experience**

**Objective:** To narrate events using past tense and sequencing.

**What to Prepare:**

- A memorable event
- Beginning-middle-end structure

**Example Topics:**

- "My Best Day"
- "A Lesson I Learned"
- "An Embarrassing Moment"

## **LEVEL 2 - DEVELOPMENT (Structure & Expression)**

_Goal: Build structured speaking & confidence_

### **Speech 1: Opinion Speech**

**Objective:** To express and justify opinions logically.

**What to Prepare:**

- Clear opinion
- 2-3 supporting points

**Example Topics:**

- "Online vs Offline Learning"
- "Is Social Media Good or Bad?"
- "Should Exams Be Removed?"

### **Speech 2: Informative Speech**

**Objective:** To explain a topic clearly and logically.

**What to Prepare:**

- Facts, examples, explanation
- Simple structure (Intro-Body-Conclusion)

**Example Topics:**

- "Artificial Intelligence Basics"
- "Importance of Time Management"
- "How to Stay Fit"

### **Speech 3: Storytelling Speech**

**Objective:** To engage the audience through narration.

**What to Prepare:**

- Characters, plot, message
- Voice modulation

**Example Topics:**

- "A Story That Inspired Me"
- "A Fictional Story with a Moral"
- "A Childhood Memory"

### **Speech 4: Problem-Solution Speech**

**Objective:** To identify a problem and propose solutions.

**What to Prepare:**

- Define a problem
- Suggest practical solutions

**Example Topics:**

- "Stress Among Students"
- "Lack of Communication Skills"
- "Mobile Addiction"

## ** LEVEL 3 - ADVANCED (Impact & Persuasion)**

_Goal: Develop influence and professional speaking_

### **Speech 1: Persuasive Speech**

**Objective:** To convince the audience effectively.

**What to Prepare:**

- Strong argument
- Emotional + logical appeal

**Example Topics:**

- "Why Communication Skills Matter More Than Marks"
- "Why Reading Should Be a Daily Habit"
- "Say No to Procrastination"

### **Speech 2: Presentation Speech**

**Objective:** To simulate corporate or academic presentations.

**What to Prepare:**

- Structured content
- Professional tone

**Example Topics:**

- "My Dream Company"
- "Startup Idea Pitch"
- "Market Trends in Finance/Marketing"

### **Speech 3: Debate Speech**

**Objective:** To argue for or against a topic confidently.

**What to Prepare:**

- Strong stance
- Counter arguments

**Example Topics:**

- "AI Will Replace Jobs"
- "Work From Home vs Office"
- "Marks Define Intelligence"

### **Speech 4: Interview Simulation Speech**

**Objective:** To prepare for real placement interviews.

**What to Prepare:**

- Common interview answers
- Confidence and clarity

**Example Topics:**

- "Tell Me About Yourself"
- "Why Should We Hire You?"
- "Strengths & Weaknesses"

## **LEVEL 4 - MASTERY (Professional & Leadership Communication)**

_Goal: Create impactful, real-world communicators_

### **Speech 1: Leadership Speech**

**Objective:** To inspire, lead, and influence an audience.

**What to Prepare:**

- Vision, motivation
- Strong opening & closing

**Example Topics:**

- "Be the Change"
- "Leadership is a Choice"
- "Rise Above Average"

### **Speech 2: Extempore / Impromptu Speech**

**Objective:** To think and speak instantly with clarity.

**What to Prepare:**

- Practice thinking frameworks (PREP method)

**Example Topics (on the spot):**

- "Success"
- "Failure"
- "Money vs Happiness"

### **Speech 3: Corporate Presentation**

**Objective:** To deliver high-level professional presentations.

**What to Prepare:**

- Data, structure, clarity
- Confident delivery

**Example Topics:**

- "Business Strategy Plan"
- "Company Analysis"
- "Product Launch Pitch"

### **Speech 4: Impact Speech (TED-style)**

**Objective:** To deliver a powerful, memorable talk.

**What to Prepare:**

- Story + message + emotion
- Audience connection

**Example Topics:**

- "Why No One Is Happy?"
- "My Biggest Failure"
- "The Power of Consistency"

# **Final Outcome of This Framework**

By Level 4, students will:  
✔ **Speak confidently in any situation  
✔ Handle interviews & presentations  
✔ Influence and inspire audiences  
✔ Become lifelong communicators**
